Chapter 5 Integers: Function Summary

1 Overview

This document reflects the current state of HoTTRijke/chapter5_ints.lean.

It provides: - A brief summary of each function. - Function signatures, shown with backticks for readability. - A separate section for possible issues. - A separate section for duplicate or very similar functions.

2 Structure

The Lean file is organized into: - Namespace Naturals: algebraic equalities for myN addition and multiplication. - Namespace Integers: algebraic equalities and helper laws for myZ addition and multiplication.

5 Possible Issues

  1. Remaining sorry placeholders are now confined to multNaturalWithZ_comm, several branches of mult_commutative, mult_associative, add_mult_right_distributive, and add_mult_left_distributive.
  2. The statement of add_mult_left_distributive still has the non-standard left-hand side (a × (b + c) × c), so the documentation preserves that exact source statement rather than normalizing it.
  3. The declaration variable (U : Unit) appears unused.
  4. The file contains both #check myAdd near the top and #print myMultZ near the end, which look like interactive debugging aids rather than theorem content.
  5. Names addNtoZ_commutative and subNfromZ_commutative are potentially misleading: they express equivalence with myAddZ, not commutativity between two symmetric operands.

6 Duplicate and Similar Functions

6.1 Potential Duplicates by Purpose

  1. left_add_one_toZpos and left_add_one_toZneg are case-split helpers consolidated by left_add_one.
  2. left_sub_one_toZpos and left_sub_one_toZneg are case-split helpers consolidated by left_sub_one.
  3. zero_add_left_neg and zero_add_left_pos are case-split helpers consolidated by zero_add_left_Z.

6.2 Very Similar (Dual) Pairs

  1. succ_pred_elim and pred_succ_elim are dual cancellation theorems.
  2. pred_succ_symm and succ_pred_symm have nearly identical structure with reversed composition order.
  3. addNtoZ_left_pred_law and subNfromZ_left_pred_law are structural analogs.
  4. addNtoZ_left_succ_law and subNfromZ_left_succ_law are structural analogs.
  5. addNtoZ_associative and subNfromZ_associative are structural analogs.

6.3 Cross-Namespace Name Reuse

  1. mult_commutative exists in both namespace Naturals and namespace Integers (different domains and signatures).
  2. mult_associative exists in both namespace Naturals and namespace Integers (different domains and signatures).
